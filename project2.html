Sales Performance Dashboard & Analytics Platform

1. Project Overview

This project is an internal, enterprise-level sales analytics and reporting platform designed to centralize and standardize sales, stock, supply chain, and production visibility across the organization. The system was built to support a highly distributed sales structure operating across multiple states in India, with a hierarchy consisting of Dealers reporting to ASMs, ASMs reporting to RSMs, and regional leadership overseeing overall performance.

The platform was implemented as a lightweight Flask-based web application on the frontend, supported by a robust and heavily automated backend ETL pipeline. While the application itself is analytical in nature rather than transactional, it processes large volumes of data daily to deliver near real-time insights to business users.

2. Business Problem

Before the implementation of this system, sales and operational data existed across multiple disconnected sources and formats, covering a wide range of product categories, subcategories, and regions. Detailed depot-level stock information, pending product details, cut-case reports, launch-pending status, and image-based product reports were generated and shared manually.

These reports were distributed via email to individual users across the organization. Due to the volume and complexity of the data, emails often included large attachments containing product images, resulting in gigabytes of data being circulated daily. This led to significant challenges related to mail server load, storage limitations, and user inbox management.

Additionally, during month-end and regional sales review meetings, presentation decks were prepared manually by sales personnel in each state. This process not only consumed substantial time but also disrupted daily operational responsibilities and introduced inconsistencies in reporting formats and metrics.

3. Solution Architecture

To address these challenges, a centralized analytics platform was designed with a clear separation between data processing and data presentation layers.

The backend consists of a fully automated ETL pipeline that integrates with SAP HANA and FTP servers. Every morning, previous-day data related to sales, stock, supply chain movements, and production is received via FTP. This data is ingested, validated, transformed, and aggregated into multiple analytical datasets.

More than 100 structured and image-enriched analytical reports are generated during this process. The processed outputs are stored both in a MySQL database for structured querying and in shared server folders for archival and external access.

The Flask application consumes only the finalized analytical outputs, ensuring that the frontend remains lightweight and responsive. Since the application is primarily analytical, the design focuses on fast data retrieval, filtering, and visualization rather than transactional workflows. Over time, selective operational features such as stock updates and allotment windows were integrated to enhance usability.

4. Key Features

• Centralized access to sales, stock, production, and supply chain analytics
• Automated daily data ingestion from SAP HANA via FTP
• Generation of 100+ standardized analytical reports with image mapping
• Hierarchical visibility for Dealers, ASMs, RSMs, and regional leadership
• Historical data retention with financial year–wise tracking
• Reduction of email-based reporting and manual PPT preparation

5. Impact & Outcomes

The implementation of this platform resulted in a significant reduction in manual effort, time consumption, and infrastructure strain. Reporting that previously required extensive email communication and manual compilation is now accessible through a centralized system.

Users can access near real-time insights instead of waiting for month-end reports, enabling proactive decision-making. The platform also maintains historical data across financial years, allowing leadership to analyze long-term trends and performance patterns.

Overall, the solution drastically reduced operational overhead, improved data consistency, eliminated redundant reporting workflows, and enabled a scalable and future-ready analytics ecosystem aligned with the organization’s growth.
