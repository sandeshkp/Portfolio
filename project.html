Production Planning Software (PPS)

1. Project Overview

The Production Planning Software (PPS) is an internal, Flask-based web application designed to support daily production and mould planning operations across manufacturing units. The application is optimized for controlled internal usage, with peak access occurring during scheduled planning windows.

The system converts dealer requirements and historical sales data into structured production plans, enabling planners to determine the appropriate mould, machine, and production sequence for each day. By automating this workflow, the application ensures consistency, accuracy, and faster decision-making in production setup.

2. Business Motivation

Prior to this system, production planning was performed manually by a small number of personnel at each manufacturing unit. Demand inputs were received from multiple states in the form of Excel sheets, followed by extensive coordination through phone calls and manual adjustments.

Frequent intervention from regional sales teams to prioritize high-demand products often resulted in abrupt production changes. This led to machine stoppages, inefficient mould changes, and operational confusion during peak hours.

A critical operational requirement was to ensure that machines operate continuously, as unplanned mould changes or idle time directly impacted productivity. Machines also vary by compatibility (PU, Non-PU, etc.), and plants operate under different layouts such as linear and circular configurations, which required systematic handling.

The overarching objective was to ensure the daily production of market-relevant products while minimizing stockouts, excess inventory, and non-moving or defective stock accumulation.

3. Impact & Outcomes

The most significant outcome of the Production Planning Software is a substantial improvement in operational efficiency and planning accuracy.

Earlier, production plans were typically finalized with a one-day delay due to manual consolidation and coordination efforts, consuming nearly half a working day.

With the introduction of PPS, production planning is now live, standardized, and data-driven, resulting in an estimated reduction of planning time by approximately 90%. The system enables uninterrupted machine utilization and provides clear visibility into upcoming production sequences.

Operators and planners can easily identify the next product to be produced, the appropriate mould, required production cycles, and machine availability. Overall, the system has contributed to improved reliability, cost efficiency, and alignment between production output and market demand.
