The Dealer Allotment Script is a production-grade Python automation built entirely in Pandas, NumPy and Jupyter Notebook, deployed within the Distribution Management System (DMS) department to govern the daily allocation of footwear products to dealers across all distribution zones.

Each morning, dealers submit their product requirements for the day through the DMS portal. The script ingests these requirements, cross-references them against real-time stock availability, applies a multi-layered rationalisation engine, and produces a finalised allotment output — which is then directly consumed by the production planning and warehouse dispatch teams to initiate the day's operations.

What Makes This Non-Trivial

On the surface this reads as a distribution problem. In practice, it is a constrained optimisation exercise: hundreds of dealers competing for limited stock across dozens of SKUs, each with different priorities, inventory classifications, market standing and outstanding dues — all resolved in a single automated script run before 9 AM every working day.

Business Problem

Prior to this script, allotment was performed manually by the DMS team using Excel sheets and telephonic coordination with warehouse and production planners. The process was:

• Time-intensive — consuming 3–4 hours of DMS team time each morning

• Inconsistent — allotment decisions varied by individual judgment, not standardised criteria

• Opaque — no audit trail for why a specific dealer received or was denied a product

• Reactive — stock priorities were not weighted by market performance or inventory class

• Error-prone — manual data entry mismatches between DMS portal and dispatch sheets

Objective

Design and deploy a fully automated allotment engine that:

• Ingests the previous day's dealer requirements from DMS portal export

• Reads live stock availability from the warehouse inventory table

• Applies multi-criteria rationalisation to resolve demand-supply gaps

• Classifies every allotment line into a Red / Green / Black tier

• Produces a finalised, production-ready allotment sheet by 9 AM

• Maintains a full audit log of every decision made by the script

Three-Tier Rationalisation System

The core innovation of this script is its traffic-light classification system. Every dealer–SKU allotment line is assigned one of three statuses based on a weighted multi-criteria scoring engine before a single unit is dispatched.

🟢 GREEN — Full Allotment
Stock is available. Dealer priority score is high. Inventory class supports dispatch. Market trend is positive for this SKU–zone combination. Full requirement quantity is allotted and flagged for immediate dispatch.
🔴 RED — Partial / Deferred
Demand exceeds available stock OR dealer priority score falls below threshold. Requirement is rationalised — partial quantity allotted based on fair-share logic. Remaining demand is queued for the next production cycle.
⚫ BLACK — Blocked
Allotment is blocked due to outstanding dues, credit limit breach, zero stock with no replenishment in pipeline, or market inactivity flag. Dealer is notified via DMS portal. No dispatch initiated.

Criteria Weights (Multi-Factor Scoring)

Dealer-Level Factors

• Priority class — A / B / C dealer tier from CRM

• Outstanding dues — overdue invoice ageing bucket

• Credit utilisation — % of credit limit consumed

• Historical offtake — trailing 30-day sales velocity

• Market activity — last order recency flag

SKU / Stock Factors

• Stock availability — warehouse live count vs. requirement

• Inventory class — fast / slow / non-moving classification

• Market trend — regional sell-through rate for the SKU

• Replenishment pipeline — production schedule visibility

• Minimum dispatch qty — carton/pair floor enforced

Step-by-Step Breakdown

Step 1 — Ingest: Previous day's dealer requirements loaded from DMS portal CSV export into a Pandas DataFrame. Schema validation checks for missing SKUs, null quantities, and unknown dealer codes.

Step 2 — Stock Merge: Live warehouse stock table (MySQL) queried via SQLAlchemy and merged on SKU key. Available stock quantity computed after reserving safety stock buffer.

Step 3 — Dealer Master Join: Dealer priority class, credit utilisation, outstanding dues and market activity flags joined from the dealer master table.

Step 4 — Composite Scoring: Each dealer–SKU row scored using a weighted NumPy dot product across all criteria columns. Rows sorted descending by composite score before allotment begins.

Step 5 — Iterative Allotment Loop: Script iterates over sorted rows. For each row, available stock is decremented by allotted quantity. Once stock reaches zero for a SKU, all remaining rows for that SKU are flagged RED or BLACK.

Step 6 — Classification: Traffic-light status assigned per row based on allotment outcome and blocking conditions.

Step 7 — Output Export: Final allotment DataFrame written to a formatted Excel file via openpyxl with conditional cell colouring (green / red / black rows) for DMS team readability.

Step 8 — Audit Log: Every decision appended to a persistent MySQL audit table with timestamp, dealer code, SKU, requested qty, allotted qty, status, and blocking reason code.

Rationalisation Criteria — Full Detail

1. Dealer Priority Class (Weight: 30%)

Each dealer is pre-classified into Tier A, B or C based on their historical annual offtake, geographic market size and relationship tenure. Tier A dealers receive first-priority access during stock scarcity. This classification is pulled live from the dealer master and encoded numerically (A = 3, B = 2, C = 1) before scoring.

2. Stock Availability Ratio (Weight: 25%)

Computed as available_stock / total_requested_across_all_dealers for each SKU. When this ratio falls below 1.0 (oversubscription), the rationalisation engine activates fair-share distribution logic — each dealer receives a proportionate allocation weighted by their composite score rank.

3. Offtake Velocity (Weight: 20%)

Trailing 30-day sell-through rate for the dealer–SKU pair, computed from historical DMS sales data. High-velocity dealer–SKU combinations are prioritised to prevent stockouts at active market points and maintain fill rates where demand is proven and recurring.

4. Outstanding Dues Flag (Weight: 15%)

Dealers with overdue invoices beyond the credit policy threshold (typically 45 days past due) receive a hard block flag. Dealers within the 15–45 day bucket receive a partial score penalty, reducing their allotment rank without full blockage. This criterion directly enforces financial discipline across the distribution network.

5. Market Trend Score (Weight: 10%)

Regional sell-through trend for the SKU over the past 60 days, normalised between 0 and 1. SKUs with declining regional sell-through are deprioritised in allotment — preventing inventory pile-up at dealers in saturated micro-markets. This signal is derived from the broader sales analytics pipeline.

Inventory Classification Overlay

• Fast Moving — High sell-through, replenished frequently. Allotted first.

• Slow Moving — Below-average velocity. Allotted only to high-priority dealers with recent order history.

• Non-Moving — Zero sell-through in 90 days. Blocked from fresh allotment. Flagged for scheme or liquidation review.

Script Output — Allotment Sheet

DMS Team

• Dealer-wise allotment with GREEN / RED / BLACK status per SKU line

• Blocking reason codes for BLACK rows (dues, credit, zero stock)

Production & Warehouse

• Consolidated GREEN allotment list fed into Production Planning Software (PPS)

• SKU-wise total allotted quantity triggers production batch sizing

• Warehouse dispatch queue generated directly from allotment output
